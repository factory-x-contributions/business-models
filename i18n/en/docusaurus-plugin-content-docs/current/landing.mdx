# Rethinking Business Models in the Data Space

Data spaces are fundamentally changing the rules of industrial value creation. For the first time, they enable secure, sovereign, and scalable collaboration across company boundaries – and with it the long-awaited scaling of novel data-driven services, products, and cooperations.

> **But the real lever is not in the technology. It lies in the business model.**

## Why Data Spaces Are Becoming Relevant Now

Industrial value creation is under pressure: increasing complexity, growing competition, and new regulatory requirements. At the same time, classic digitalization approaches are reaching their limits – isolated solutions and proprietary platforms can hardly be scaled.

Data spaces address exactly this challenge: they create a shared infrastructure for multilateral data sharing without giving up control over your own data. This opens up new possibilities:

## The Opportunity: New Business Models Are Emerging

New perspectives are opening up for companies – from bilateral relationships to ecosystems with many partners.

This is exemplified in mechanical and plant engineering: instead of only delivering machines, Condition Monitoring, Predictive Maintenance, or performance-based services are increasingly possible – based on operational data from the data space.

Data spaces thus enable what was previously hardly scalable: data-driven business models across company boundaries. In principle, three ways of unlocking new or additional revenue streams are conceivable:

At the same time, this opportunity exists not only for providers of new services. Companies that primarily want to use offerings in the data space also benefit – through more efficient processes, better transparency, and access to new services. However, to realize these potentials, they too must evolve their business models, for example in how they source, integrate, and evaluate services or how they shape collaboration and value contributions in the ecosystem.

## The Challenge: Business Model Innovation Is Not Automatic

<div style={{borderLeft: '4px solid #e6a817', background: 'var(--ifm-color-warning-contrast-background, #fff8e6)', borderRadius: '0 8px 8px 0', padding: '1rem 1.25rem', margin: '1rem 0'}}>
  <strong>⚠️ As great as the opportunity is – it is not trivial to realize.</strong>
  <p style={{margin: '0.5rem 0 0'}}>Data space-based business models change not only products but the entire company – regardless of whether it acts as a provider or a user in the data space.</p>
</div>

Concretely, this means changes on multiple levels:

- New roles in the value creation network
- Changes in customer and supplier relationships
- New forms of service integration and accountability

Many companies underestimate this dimension. Even simply using data-based services in the data space is not a plug-and-play approach. It requires adjustments to your own business model – in the organization, in processes, or in the evaluation of external services and data.

Business models cannot simply be extended. They require a systematic rethinking of value creation, roles, and monetization – for both providers and users in the data space.
