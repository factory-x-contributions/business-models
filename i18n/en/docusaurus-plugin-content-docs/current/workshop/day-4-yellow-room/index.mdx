<DaySection id="dd1" time="09:30 - 10:45" title="Value Source & Willingness to Pay">

### Goal

Understand **what the customer pays for** and quantify this value in EUR.

<Admonition type="info" title="Important">
Customers pay for **results**, not for features. Link the price to measurable benefit.
</Admonition>

### Types of Value Sources

| Value Source | Description | Example |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| **Savings** | Cost reduction at the customer | Fewer downtimes, lower maintenance costs |
| **Additional Revenue** | Enable additional revenues | Higher productivity |
| **Risk Reduction** | Avoidance of damages | Fewer failures |
| **Time Savings** | Faster processes | Shorter reaction times |
| **Compliance** | Fulfill documentation requirements | Documentation |

### Value Quantification (Example)

| Customer Benefit | Quantification | Calculation |
|------------------|----------------|-------------|
| Downtimes reduced | 40% less | 10 failures/year → 6 failures/year |
| Downtime costs | 10,000 EUR/h | 4 avoided failures × 4h × 10,000 EUR |
| **Value per Year** | | **160,000 EUR** |

### KPIs for Value Measurement

| KPI | Type | Target Group |
|-----|------|--------------|
| **Unplanned Downtime** (h/month) | Primary | Management |
| Asset Availability (%) | Secondary | Operations |
| MTTR (Mean Time to Repair) | Secondary | Maintenance |
| Maintenance Costs (EUR/machine) | Secondary | Controlling |

### Facilitation Questions

- "For which concrete value does the customer pay?"
- "Rather 5% or 30% cost reduction - what is realistic?"
- "How can we make the value measurable?"

</DaySection>

<DaySection id="dd2" time="11:00 - 12:30" title="Revenue Mechanics & Pricing Model">

### Goal

Choose the **appropriate revenue model** for the offering.

### Revenue Models Overview

| Model | Description | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|-------|-------------|------------|---------------|
| **Subscription** | Regular fee | Predictable revenues | Churn risk |
| **Pay-per-Use** | Usage-based | Fair, scales | Variable revenues |
| **Outcome-Based** | Payment by result | Strong value alignment | Risk with provider |
| **Freemium** | Basic free | Low barrier | Conversion needed |
| **License** | One-time payment | High immediate revenue | No recurring |
| **Hybrid** | Combination | Flexible | Complexity |

### Pricing Model Components

| Component | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Fixed Component** | Base fee | Subscription per machine/month |
| **Variable Component** | Usage-dependent | Per alert, per report |
| **Setup Fee** | One-time | Integration, onboarding |
| **Add-ons** | Optional extensions | Advanced Analytics |

### Approach (90 Min)

1. **Evaluate Revenue Models** (30 Min): Which model fits?
2. **Define Price Components** (30 Min): Fixed, variable, setup
3. **Set Price Points** (20 Min): Initial price proposals
4. **Validate** (10 Min): Is this acceptable to the customer?

### Facilitation Questions

- "Which revenue mechanics fits the customer benefit?"
- "Which price drivers are fair and comprehensible?"
- "Is the pricing logic simple enough for sales?"

</DaySection>

<DaySection id="dd3" time="13:30 - 14:45" title="Cost Structure">

### Goal

Document all **relevant cost blocks** - honestly and completely.

### Cost Types

| Category | Examples | Behavior |
|----------|----------|----------|
| **Development** | Software, models, integration | One-time/project |
| **Infrastructure** | Cloud, server, network | Fixed + variable |
| **Personnel** | Support, operations, sales | Fixed |
| **Partners** | Commissions, licenses | Variable |
| **Data Space** | Connector fees, registry | Fixed + variable |
| **Marketing** | Sales, onboarding | Variable |

### Cost Structure Template

| Cost Block | Fixed/Variable | One-time/Ongoing | Estimate |
|------------|----------------|------------------|----------|
| Initial Development | Fixed | One-time | X EUR |
| Cloud Infrastructure | Variable | Ongoing | Y EUR/month |
| Support Personnel | Fixed | Ongoing | Z EUR/month |
| Data Space Fees | Fixed + Var | Ongoing | A EUR/month |
| Customer Acquisition | Variable | Per customer | B EUR/customer |

### Facilitation Questions

- "Which costs are fixed vs. variable?"
- "Where do we need partners & SLAs?"
- "How do costs behave with scaling?"

</DaySection>

<DaySection id="dd4" time="15:00 - 16:30" title="Synthesis: Business Case">

### Goal

Evaluate economic viability and calculate break-even.

### Business Case Structure

| Element | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Revenues** | | | |
| Number of Customers | 5 | 15 | 30 |
| Machines/Customer | 3 | 5 | 8 |
| Revenue/Machine/Year | X EUR | X EUR | X EUR |
| **Total Revenues** | A EUR | B EUR | C EUR |
| **Costs** | | | |
| Fixed Costs | Y EUR | Y EUR | Y EUR |
| Variable Costs | Z EUR | Z EUR | Z EUR |
| **Total Costs** | D EUR | E EUR | F EUR |
| **Result** | A-D | B-E | C-F |

### Break-even Analysis

| Metric | Calculation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Break-even Point** | Fixed costs / (Revenue - var. costs per unit) |
| **Payback Period** | Investment / annual cash flow |
| **ROI** | (Profit - Investment) / Investment |

### Scenario Analysis

| Scenario | Assumptions | Break-even |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Best Case** | Fast adoption, high prices | 12 months |
| **Base Case** | Realistic adoption | 18 months |
| **Worst Case** | Slow adoption, price pressure | 30 months |

<Admonition type="info" title="Key Insight">
The decisive lever is often **not the price**, but **scaling via number of machines per customer**. Volume beats price optimization.
</Admonition>

### Facilitation Questions

- "What goals do we pursue with the pricing strategy?"
- "Which pilots can we use to test the model?"
- "What are no-gos in pricing?"

</DaySection>

<AiSection title="AI Support (Hybrid Format)">

In the hybrid format, AI can support the economic analysis:

| Session | AI Use | How |
|---------|--------|-----|
| **Value Quantification** | Benefit Calculation | AI helps quantify savings and ROI |
| **Revenue Mechanics** | Model Comparison | AI analyzes pros/cons of different pricing models |
| **Cost Structure** | Cost Identification | AI identifies often forgotten cost blocks |
| **Business Case** | Scenario Calculation | AI creates calculations and sensitivity analyses |

### Specific AI Prompts

**Value Quantification:**
> "Calculate the economic value for a customer if [pain] is reduced by X%. Assumptions: [downtime costs], [frequency], [industry]. Show the calculation."

**Pricing Model Analysis:**
> "Compare subscription vs. pay-per-use vs. outcome-based pricing for [offering]. Which model fits [customer type]? What are risks and opportunities?"

**Cost Structure:**
> "What cost blocks arise when building and operating a [service] offering? Distinguish development, infrastructure, personnel, partners, marketing. What are typical magnitudes?"

**Business Case:**
> "Create a 3-year business case for [offering] with: [price], [estimated customers year 1/2/3], [fixed costs], [variable costs per customer]. Calculate break-even and ROI."

<Admonition type="tip" title="Best Practice">
AI can **quickly calculate** and compare scenarios. The team provides assumptions, AI calculates and visualizes. Particularly useful for sensitivity analyses.
</Admonition>

</AiSection>
