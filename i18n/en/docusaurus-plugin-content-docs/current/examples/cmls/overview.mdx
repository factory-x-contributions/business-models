## Business Idea at a Glance

A **machine builder** wants to build a service-based business model beyond pure machine sales. The goal is the **preventive and reliable maintenance** of their machines at customer sites – through systematic analysis of machine components, assessment of failure probability, and definition of preventive measures.

| Dimension | Content |
|---|---|
| **Customer & Need** | Small and medium-sized manufacturing companies (SMEs) in Europe, individual and small-batch production. Core problems: unplanned downtime, reactive maintenance, lack of data transparency, skills shortage |
| **Value Proposition** | Data-driven Condition Monitoring Service: continuous machine monitoring, risk assessment, automated recommendations for preventive maintenance |
| **Value Creation** | Data collection (sensor & operational data) → Data integration via data space → Analysis (condition assessment, wear prognosis) → Visualization & communication → Spare parts & service logistics |
| **Monetization** | Subscription-based service packages (Basic Monitoring, Advanced Analytics, Spare Parts Service), Pay-per-Use for special analyses, follow-on revenue from spare parts sales and field services |

| **Starting Point** | Growing requirements for digitalization and efficiency in manufacturing. Competitive advantage through high-margin service business and lifecycle-based customer retention |

## Executive Summary

<Admonition type="info" title="Business Model Blueprint – Executive Summary">

In the considered business model, a **machine building company** as a manufacturer of machine tools pursues the goal of offering data-based services to users of its machines and thereby generating additional business through an extended value proposition. The aim is to **reduce unplanned downtime** and make plant operations more economically transparent.

**Target customers** are factory operators of small to medium size (SMEs) with production sites in high-wage countries (Germany / Europe), a mid-range parts spectrum, and significant small-batch and single-unit production. Their central challenges lie in unplanned downtime, expensive ad-hoc maintenance, and a lack of condition/cause transparency – exacerbated by **skills shortage**, heterogeneous IT/OT landscapes, and the volatility of small-batch and single-unit production.

The data-based service delivers end-to-end **Condition Monitoring with Analytics**, clear alerts and guided diagnostics, measurably reduces unplanned downtime, and makes decisions controllable via KPIs (including downtime, availability, MTTR, maintenance effort).

</Admonition>

### Service Offering Overview

The concrete service offering is structured as a clear **service modular system**:

<details>
<summary>One-time services: Setup & Enablement</summary>
<div>

1. **Scope definition & KPI measurement concept** – 1–3 machines, KPI definition (unplanned downtime as primary KPI), baseline measurement
2. **Data connection & technical commissioning** – Machine connection, configuration of data flows, data quality check as go-live gate
3. **Monitoring & alerting configuration** – Dashboards for pilot machines, initial thresholds/rules for alerts
4. **Process & role enablement** – Escalation logic (alert → diagnosis → decision → action), training

</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Recurring services: Operation of the digital service</summary>
<div>

5. **Continuous condition monitoring** – Ongoing condition monitoring, transparency over condition histories and events
6. **Analytics & diagnostic support** – Anomaly detection, trend and pattern recognition for wear/failure patterns
7. **Recommendations & maintenance support** – Action proposals, shift from reactive to condition-based maintenance
8. **Service/support assistance** – Remote-support capable, structured support process, MTTR reduction
9. **Reporting & value proof** – Monthly/quarterly KPI reports, before/after comparison (Proof of Value)

</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary>Optional extensions: Scaling & Ecosystem</summary>
<div>

10. **Integration into service and spare parts logistics** – Interface to scheduling/ordering support (Insight → Action without media breaks)
11. **Ecosystem/partner capability** – White-label logic, data space/ecosystem conformity
12. **Scaling package** – Standardized rollout packages (1–3 → 5–10 → 20+ machines)

</div>
</details>

## Economics at a Glance

<Admonition type="tip" title="Unit Economics – Reference Model (10 machines, model-based)">

| KPI | Value |
|---|---|
| Revenue/month | €11,000 (€800 Basic + €300 Advanced Analytics × 10 machines) |
| Fixed costs/month | €4,000 (platform, support, dev, overhead) |
| Variable costs/month | €2,200 (€220 × 10 machines) |
| **Contribution margin/month** | **€4,800 (~44%)** |
| Break-even | ~5 machines (model-based) |
| Strongest scaling lever | Number of machines per customer |

*All figures are model assumptions, not actual costs. Price variations ±20% and cost increases +30% keep the model positive at 10 machines.*

</Admonition>
